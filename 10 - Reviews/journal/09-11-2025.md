### Phase 1: Foundation & Team Assembly (Weeks 1-4)

1.  **Formalize Core Team Roles:**
    *   **Project Lead (You):** Oversee the entire project, vision, and execution.
    *   **Curriculum Developer:** Recruit an expert to research and structure the educational content based on established principles (Vygotsky, HEAL Framework, etc.) and modern AI advancements.
    *   **Partnerships Coordinator:** Assign a person to formalize collaborations with institutions like NHREF, Johns Hopkins, and local Medical Faculties.
    *   **Finance & Documentation Officer:** Appoint someone to manage the budget and document all processes.
    *   **Marketing & Branding Lead:** Find a lead to shape the program's public image and outreach strategy.

2.  **Establish Partnerships:**
    *   Draft and sign Memorandums of Understanding (MOUs) with NHREF, Johns Hopkins, and Medical Faculties to define roles, responsibilities, and access to student groups for the pilot program.

3.  **Set Up Infrastructure:**
    *   Deploy a cloud-based backend (e.g., using AWS S3 as mentioned in your notes) for secure project storage and documentation.

### Phase 2: Curriculum & Content Development (Weeks 5-12)

1.  **Develop Core Curriculum:**
    *   **Basic Course (for First-Year Students):**
        *   **Module 1: Learning Concepts:** Based on `A Knowledge-Rich Curriculum`, `Future-proof Learning`, and `6 strategies for learning`.
        *   **Module 2: Memory & Retention:** Incorporate principles from `The Role of Long-Term Memory in Learning` and the HEAL framework.
        *   **Module 3: Metacognition & Mindfulness:** Integrate content from `Metacognition in the Age of AI` and the Buddhist Mindfulness pillar.
    *   **Advanced Course (for Senior Students & Professionals):**
        *   **Module 4: AI Integration & Tools:** Focus on practical application, leveraging AI for clinical reasoning and practice.
        *   **Module 5: Clinical Reasoning:** Develop simulations and case studies based on `Cognitive Biases`, `Pattern Recognition`, and `Teaching Clinical Reasoning`.

2.  **Create Educational Content:**
    *   Generate initial presentations, pamphlets, and online materials for both courses.
    *   Utilize AI agents (like Claude) for drafting emails, generating marketing copy, and creating presentation outlines.

3.  **Develop Assessment Framework:**
    *   Design quizzes and reflection essays for the Basic Course.
    *   Create specifications for AI-driven simulations and case-based assessments for the Advanced Course.

### Phase 3: Platform & Tooling (Weeks 13-20)

1.  **Build/Integrate AI Practice Tools:**
    *   **Smart Flashcards:** Develop an AI-powered flashcard system.
    *   **Virtual Patient Sims:** Create interactive virtual patient scenarios for diagnostic practice.
    *   **Clinical Rehearsal AI:** Build a tool for students to rehearse clinical interactions and receive feedback.

2.  **Establish Student Feedback Loop:**
    *   Design surveys, feedback forms, and a communication channel for pilot participants.

3.  **Develop the Marketing Website/Platform:**
    *   Create a central online hub for the program with course information, registration, and access to learning materials.

### Phase 4: Marketing & Pilot Program (Weeks 21-26)

1.  **Launch Marketing Campaign:**
    *   Deploy the marketing team to create branding assets.
    *   Begin outreach to the target audience through partner institutions.
    *   Use AI-generated content for social media, email newsletters, and blog posts.

2.  **Recruit for Pilot Program:**
    *   Select a cohort of students from partner medical faculties to participate in the initial pilot.

3.  **Run Pilot Program:**
    *   Execute the Basic and Advanced courses with the pilot group.
    *   Gather intensive feedback through the established feedback loop.

### Phase 5: Refinement & Public Launch (Weeks 27-30)

1.  **Analyze Pilot Feedback & Iterate:**
    *   Refine the curriculum, content, and AI tools based on feedback from the pilot cohort.
    *   Address any bugs or usability issues in the platform.

2.  **Prepare for Public Launch:**
    *   Finalize all marketing materials.
    *   Open registration to the public.

3.  **Official Launch:**
    *   Formally launch "The Infinite Learner" program.
    *   Host a launch event or webinar to introduce the program to a wider audience.

### Phase 6: Ongoing Operation & Evolution (Post-Launch)

1.  **Continuous Improvement:**
    *   The Curriculum Developer continues to research the latest in AI and education to keep the content up-to-date.
    *   Regularly collect and analyze student feedback to iterate on the program.

2.  **Scale & Expand:**
    *   Explore opportunities to expand partnerships with other institutions.
    *   Develop new, specialized courses based on demand.
