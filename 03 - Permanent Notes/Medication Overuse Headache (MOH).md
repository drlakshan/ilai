# Medication Overuse Headache (MOH)

## Definition
**Medication Overuse Headache** is a paradoxical increase in headache frequency caused by the regular, excessive use of acute headache medications.

## Pathophysiology
- **Frequent medication use** transforms episodic migraine into chronic pattern
- **Rebound phenomenon** when medication wears off
- **Central sensitization** from repeated drug exposure

## Risk Thresholds

### Triptans
- **Risk threshold**: ≥10 days per month
- **High risk**: Daily use
- **Transformation**: Episodic → Chronic migraine

### Other Acute Medications
- **NSAIDs**: ≥15 days per month
- **Combination analgesics**: ≥10 days per month
- **Opioids**: ≥10 days per month
- **Butalbital-containing**: ≥5 days per month

## Clinical Features
- **Headache frequency**: ≥15 days per month
- **Medication dependency**: Need for daily medication
- **Withdrawal symptoms**: Worsening without medication
- **Poor response**: Decreasing efficacy of acute treatments

## Prevention Strategies

### Patient Education
- **Clear limits** on acute medication use
- **Understanding of MOH** concept
- **Headache diary** monitoring
- **Preventive therapy** when appropriate

### Treatment Approach
1. **Medication withdrawal** (gradual vs abrupt)
2. **Bridge therapy** during withdrawal
3. **Preventive medications**
4. **Lifestyle modifications**

## Bridge Therapies
- **Steroids**: Short course during withdrawal
- **Long-acting NSAIDs**: Naproxen
- **Neuromodulation**: Non-pharmacologic options

## Clinical Pearls
- **Screen all chronic headache patients** for MOH
- **Set clear limits** from initial consultation
- **Use headache diaries** to monitor frequency
- **Consider preventive therapy** early in high-risk patients
