### Health Risks of Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)
*   **Thesis:** OSA is an independent risk factor for significant cardiovascular, metabolic, and neurobehavioral morbidities, and is associated with increased all-cause mortality.
*   **Key Points:**
    *   Cardiovascular risks include hypertension and other cardiovascular diseases.
    *   Metabolic risks include Type II diabetes.
    *   Neurobehavioral risks include daytime sleepiness, impaired cognitive function, and increased risk of accidents.
    *   Aggravating factors include obesity, alcohol, smoking, and certain craniofacial features.
