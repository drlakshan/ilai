# Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)

## Pathophysiology
Systemic autoimmune disease defined by **classic triad**:
1. Necrotizing granulomatous inflammation (respiratory tract)
2. Necrotizing vasculitis (small-to-medium vessels)
3. Glomerulonephritis

**Serology**: Strongly associated with **c-ANCA** (anti-PR3)

## Sinonasal Manifestations
**Frequency**: Up to 90% of patients (often initial presentation)

**Symptoms**:
- Bloody nasal discharge
- Extensive crusting
- Ulceration
- Septal perforation
- **Saddle nose deformity**

## Cutaneous Manifestations
**Frequency**: ~50% of patients
**Most Characteristic**: Palpable purpura (lower extremities)
**Also**: Subcutaneous nodules, ulcers

## Systemic Involvement
**Lungs**: Nodules, hemorrhage
**Kidneys**: Rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis
**Risk**: Organ failure if untreated

## Treatment
**Induction**:
- High-dose corticosteroids
- Rituximab or cyclophosphamide

**Maintenance**: Long-term immunosuppression

## Critical Differential
Must distinguish from:
- [[Extranodal NK-T Cell Lymphoma]]
- [[Cocaine-Induced Midline Destructive Lesions]]
- [[Sarcoidosis]]
