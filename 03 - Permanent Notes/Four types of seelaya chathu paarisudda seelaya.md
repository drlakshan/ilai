#indrakheelaya


There are four types of seelaya or morality 

1. Paathimokka sanwara seelaya
2. Indriya sanwara seelaya
3. Aajeewa parisuddi seelaya
4. Prathya sannisitha seelaya

# Reference

චතු පාරිශුද්ධ සීලය (Chatu Parisuddha Sila) refers to the Four Purities of Moral Discipline in Buddhism. This concept encompasses four essential aspects of ethical conduct that are crucial for spiritual development and progress towards enlightenment[1][4]. The four components of චතු පාරිශුද්ධ සීලය are:

1. **පාතිමොක්ඛසංවර සීලය** (Patimokkha-samvara-sila): This is the discipline of restraint according to the monastic code. It involves following the rules and regulations set for Buddhist monks and nuns[1][2].

2. **ඉන්ද්‍රිය සංවර සීලය** (Indriya-samvara-sila): This refers to the discipline of restraint of the senses. It involves controlling and guarding one's sensory faculties to avoid unwholesome mental states[1][2].

3. **ආජීවපාරිසුද්ධි සීලය** (Ajivaparisuddhi-sila): This is the discipline of purity of livelihood. It emphasizes maintaining an honest and ethical means of sustenance[1][2].

4. **පච්චයසන්නිස්සිත සීලය** (Paccayasannissita-sila): This relates to the discipline of proper use of the requisites of life. It involves using material possessions mindfully and with moderation[1][5].

The චතු පාරිශුද්ධ සීලය is considered a fundamental aspect of Buddhist practice, particularly for monastics. It forms the first of the Seven Purifications (සප්ත විශුද්ධි) in Buddhism, known as සීල විසුද්ධිය (Sila Visuddhi) or purification of virtue[4]. By observing these four aspects of moral discipline, practitioners aim to purify their conduct and create a strong foundation for further spiritual development.

For Buddhist monks, the practice of චතු පාරිශුද්ධ සීලය is seen as an essential part of their daily life and spiritual training. It helps them maintain a high standard of ethical behavior and supports their progress on the path to enlightenment[5][6].
