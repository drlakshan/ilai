# ENT Role in Migraine Management

## The Therapeutic Void
A **substantial proportion of patients** in ENT clinics suffer from **undiagnosed migraine**, creating a therapeutic void that otolaryngologists are uniquely positioned to fill.

## Why ENTs Should Manage Migraine

### Atypical Presentations
Migraine often presents to ENTs **without primary headache complaint**:
- **Vertigo and dizziness**
- **Facial pain and pressure**
- **Ear fullness and tinnitus**
- **Nasal congestion and rhinorrhea**

### Unique Position
- **First-line contact** for many migraine patients
- **Expertise in differential diagnosis** of ENT symptoms
- **Ability to rule out structural causes**

## ENT as Primary Migraine Manager

### Diagnostic Capabilities
- **Clinical history expertise**
- **Pattern recognition** for atypical presentations
- **Access to audiometric/vestibular testing**
- **Ability to perform ENT-specific procedures**

### Treatment Initiation
- **First-line preventive therapies**
- **Protocol-driven approach**
- **"Start low, go slow" principle**
- **Patient education and counseling**

## Referral Criteria to Neurology

### When to Refer
1. **Diagnostic uncertainty**
2. **Red flag symptoms** (SNOOPP mnemonic) [[What does SNOOP acronym stand for in Headaches]]  
3. **Treatment failure**: 2-3 failed preventive medications
4. **Complex comorbidities**
5. **Need for advanced therapies**:
   - Botox injections
   - CGRP monoclonal antibodies
   - Refractory medication overuse headache

### Red Flags (SNOOPP Mnemonic)
- **S**ystemic symptoms (fever, weight loss)
- **N**eurologic symptoms (confusion, weakness)
- **O**nset (sudden, thunderclap)
- **O**lder age of onset (>50)
- **P**attern change/progressive
- **P**ositional, precipitated by cough/sneeze

## Clinical Benefits
- **Prevents years of patient suffering**
- **Reduces misdirected treatments**
- **Prevents unnecessary sinus procedures**
- **Improves patient outcomes**
- **Reduces healthcare costs**

## Professional Imperative
There is a **professional imperative** for otolaryngologists to:
- **Diagnose atypical migraine presentations**
- **Initiate first-line management**
- **Fill the therapeutic void**
- **Improve patient care**
