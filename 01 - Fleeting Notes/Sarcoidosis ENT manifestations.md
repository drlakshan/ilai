# Sarcoidosis - ENT Manifestations

## Pathophysiology
Multisystem inflammatory disorder of unknown cause
**Hallmark**: Well-formed, **non-caseating** epithelioid granulomas

## Sinonasal Manifestations
**Frequency**: Minority of patients
**Pattern**: **Infiltrative, NOT destructive** (key differentiator)

**Appearance**:
- Swollen, boggy mucosa
- Nodular or "cobblestoned" appearance

**What Does NOT Occur** (contrast with GPA):
- Septal perforation (distinctly uncommon)
- Saddle nose deformity (distinctly uncommon)

## Cutaneous Manifestations
**Frequency**: 25% of patients

**Specific Lesions** (contain granulomas):
- Papules
- Plaques
- **Lupus pernio** (pathognomonic): Violaceous, indurated plaques on central face

**Non-specific Lesions**:
- Erythema nodosum: Tender red nodules on shins

## Systemic Involvement
**Lungs & Intrathoracic Lymph Nodes**: >90% of cases
**Also**: Eyes, joints, liver, heart, nervous system

## Diagnosis
Requires **three components**:
1. Compatible clinical picture
2. Histopathological evidence of non-caseating granulomas
3. **Exclusion of other granulomatous diseases**
