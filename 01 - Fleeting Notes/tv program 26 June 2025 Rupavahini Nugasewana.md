### Program Structure (15 minutes)

**1. Introduction (1 minute)**
*   Introduction by the host.
*   My introduction: Consultant ENT Surgeon.
*   Topic: "Common ENT problems we see in our daily practice."
*   Goal: Help viewers identify common issues and know when it's important to see a doctor.

**2. Nasal Blockage: More than just a stuffy nose (3 minutes)**
*   **Problem:** Many people suffer from long-term nasal blockage.
*   **Common Causes:**
    *   Deviated Nasal Septum (DNS): Briefly explain what it is.
    *   Nasal Polyps: Briefly explain.
*   **Audiovisual:** Simple diagram showing a deviated septum vs. a normal septum, and another showing polyps.
*   **Advice:** When to try simple remedies (like steam inhalation) vs. when to consult a specialist (if it's persistent, one-sided, or with other symptoms). 

**3. Ear Discharge & Hearing Loss (3 minutes)**
*   **Problem:** A discharging ear is a sign of an underlying issue.
*   **Common Cause:** Perforated eardrum (hole in the eardrum).
*   **Audiovisual:** Diagram of a normal eardrum vs. a perforated one.
*   **Solution:** Briefly mention Myringoplasty as a surgical option to repair the drum and improve hearing.
*   **Key Message:** Don't ignore a discharging ear; it can lead to complications.

**4. Voice Change: When to worry (2 minutes)**
*   **Problem:** Hoarseness or a change in voice.
*   **Common Causes:** Vocal strain, infections, or sometimes more serious issues.
*   **Advice (Vocal Hygiene):**
    *   Rest your voice.
    *   Stay hydrated.
    *   Avoid shouting.
*   **Red Flag:** If voice change persists for more than 3 weeks, see an ENT surgeon.

**5. Dizziness and Ringing in the Ears (Tinnitus & Vertigo) (4 minutes)**
*   **Problem:** Tinnitus (ringing) and Vertigo (spinning dizziness) are common and distressing.
*   **Explanation:**
    *   Briefly explain what they are.
    *   Mention they are often symptoms, not diseases themselves.
    *   Common causes can be related to the inner ear.
*   **Audiovisual:** Simple diagram of the inner ear balance organs.
*   **Advice:** It's a complex area, and a proper medical evaluation is needed to find the cause.

**6. Conclusion & Summary (2 minutes)**
*   **Recap:** Quickly summarize the 4 main topics discussed.
*   **Final Message:** These are common problems, but early consultation can prevent long-term issues. Don't be afraid to see a doctor.
*   **Follow-up:** Mention that viewers can follow up with questions through the program's official channels.
