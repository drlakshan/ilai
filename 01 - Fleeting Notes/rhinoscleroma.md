# Rhinoscleroma

## Etiology
Klebsiella rhinoscleromatis
**Endemic Regions**: Africa, Southeast Asia, Central/South America

## Three Clinical Stages

### 1. Catarrhal Stage
- Mimics chronic rhinitis
- Foul-smelling purulent discharge
- Crusting

### 2. Granulomatous Stage
- **Painless, rubbery, reddish granulomatous nodules**
- Form in nasal submucosa
- Severe nasal obstruction
- **No ulceration** (key differentiator)

### 3. Sclerotic Stage
- Granulomatous tissue â†’ dense scar tissue
- Stenosis
- Permanent deformities

## Pathognomonic Histology
**Mikulicz cells**: Large, vacuolated macrophages containing bacilli
**Russell bodies**: Degenerated plasma cells

## Clinical Pearl
Nodular, obstructing lesions **without** ulceration in endemic area traveler
