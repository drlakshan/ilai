# Mucormycosis (Black Fungus)

## Etiology & Epidemiology
**Causative Organisms**: Molds of order Mucorales (Rhizopus spp.)
**Host**: Immunocompromised

**Major Risk Factors**:
- Poorly controlled diabetes (especially DKA)
- Neutropenia
- Organ transplant
- Long-term steroid use

## Clinical Presentation
**Tempo**: Rapidly progressive (fulminant)
**Common Sites**: Sinuses, brain, eyes

**Symptoms**:
- One-sided facial swelling
- Headache
- Fever
- **Rapidly progressing black necrotic lesions** on nose/palate

## Pathophysiology - Angioinvasion
**Key Mechanism**: Fungus grows into blood vessels
- Causes thrombosis
- Extensive tissue necrosis
- Contrast with GIFRS (mass effect, not angioinvasion)

## Diagnosis
**Biopsy Reveals**:
- Wide, ribbon-like, **non-septate** hyphae
- Branching at **right angles**

## Treatment - URGENT
1. **Aggressive surgical debridement** of all necrotic tissue
2. Systemic antifungal: **Amphotericin B**
