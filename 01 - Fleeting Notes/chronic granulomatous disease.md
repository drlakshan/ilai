# Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)

## Pathology
**Type**: Primary immunodeficiency disorder
**Genetic Defect**: Mutations in NADPH oxidase enzyme complex

**Functional Consequence**:
- Phagocytes cannot generate reactive oxygen species
- Cannot kill **catalase-positive** bacteria and fungi

## Clinical Presentation
**Dual Pathology**:
1. **Recurrent, life-threatening infections** (bacterial, fungal)
2. **Chronic dysregulated inflammation** â†’ granuloma formation

### Sinonasal Symptoms
- Persistent rhinitis
- Frequent sinus infections

### Cutaneous Manifestations
**Infectious Lesions**:
- Abscesses
- Cellulitis

**Sterile Inflammatory Lesions**:
- Papules
- Pustules

## Diagnosis
**Confirmatory Test**: Functional assays of neutrophil oxidative burst
- **DHR (Dihydrorhodamine) test**
