# Extranodal NK-T Cell Lymphoma, Nasal Type

## Pathology
**Cell Lineage**: NK-cell or T-cell
**Viral Association**: Strongly associated with **Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)**
**Historical Name**: "Lethal midline granuloma"
**Behavior**: Highly aggressive malignancy

## Key Pathological Feature
**Angiocentric and angiodestructive**
→ Causes extensive ischemic necrosis

## Clinical Presentation
**Typical Pattern**: Relentlessly progressive, destructive midfacial lesion

**Evolution**:
1. Begins like chronic sinusitis
2. Evolves into necrotic tumor mass
3. Destroys nasal septum, palate, sinuses
4. Severe disfigurement

## Diagnosis
**Requires**: Deep tissue biopsy

**Histology**:
- Angiocentric infiltrate of atypical lymphoid cells
- Prominent necrosis

**Confirmatory Tests**:
- Immunohistochemistry: **CD56+**
- **EBER in-situ hybridization** for EBV

## Critical Diagnostic Challenge
**Must Differentiate From**: [[Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)]]

**Consequence of Misdiagnosis**:
- Treating lymphoma with immunosuppressants (GPA treatment) → accelerates tumor growth
- **CATASTROPHIC**
