# Granulomatous Invasive Fungal Rhinosinusitis (GIFRS)

## Etiology & Epidemiology
**Causative Organism**: Aspergillus flavus (most common)
**Host**: Immunocompetent (key differentiator)
**Geography**: Arid, tropical regions (India, Pakistan, Sudan)

## Clinical Presentation
**Tempo**: Indolent, slowly progressive (months to years)
**Symptoms**:
- Expanding, unilateral mass
- Nasal obstruction
- Facial swelling
- Proptosis (bulging eye)

**Clinical Mimicry**: Often mistaken for malignancy

## Unique Pathophysiology
**Not typical fungal invasion**:
- Hyper-responsive but ineffective T-cell granulomatous reaction
- Exuberant inflammation forms space-occupying lesion
- "Invades" by **mass effect**, not angioinvasion
- Contrast with mucormycosis

## Histopathology
- Extensive **non-caseating** granulomas
- **Sparse** fungal hyphae (often within giant cells)
- **Absence** of significant vascular invasion

## Treatment
Combination required:
1. Radical surgical debridement
2. Prolonged systemic antifungals (itraconazole, voriconazole)
