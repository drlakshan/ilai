# Leprosy - Sinonasal and Cutaneous Manifestations

## Etiology
Mycobacterium leprae (Hansen's Disease)

## Clinical Spectrum
**Tuberculoid**: Strong immune response, few lesions
**Lepromatous**: Weak immune response, widespread disease

## Sinonasal Manifestations
**Frequency**: Up to 95% of patients (especially lepromatous form)

**Early Symptoms**:
- Chronic nasal stuffiness
- Crusting

**Progressive Changes**:
- Chronic granulomatous infiltration
- Mucosal atrophy
- Perforation of **cartilaginous** septum
- Collapse of support structures
- **Classic "saddle nose" deformity**

## Cutaneous Manifestations
**Hallmark**: Skin lesions with **definite loss of sensation**

**Tuberculoid Form**:
- Few lesions
- Well-demarcated

**Lepromatous Form**:
- Numerous lesions
- Symmetrically distributed
- Diffuse facial infiltration â†’ **"leonine facies"**
- Loss of eyebrows and eyelashes (madarosis)

## Diagnosis
**Primarily clinical**:
- Anesthetic skin lesions
- Thickened peripheral nerves

**Biopsy Findings**:
- Tuberculoid: Well-formed granulomas
- Lepromatous: Foamy macrophages (Virchow cells) filled with acid-fast bacilli
