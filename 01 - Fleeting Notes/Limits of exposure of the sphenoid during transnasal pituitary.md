What are Sphenoethmoidal (Onodi) cells and why are they surgically significant?
They are posterior ethmoid cells that, if unrecognized, increase the risk of anterior skull base penetration or injury to the optic nerve and carotid artery. (Source: [[Transsphenoidal Surgery - Anatomy, Approaches, and Risks]])

The radiation dose limit for the pituitary and hypothalamus is set at ==<40 Gy==. (Source: [[Transsphenoidal Surgery - Anatomy, Approaches, and Risks]])
