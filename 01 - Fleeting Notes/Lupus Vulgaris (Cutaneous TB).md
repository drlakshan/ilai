# Lupus Vulgaris (Cutaneous TB)

## Definition
Most common form of cutaneous tuberculosis

## Clinical Presentation
**Location**: Head and neck predominantly
**Appearance**:
- Reddish-brown papules
- Coalesce into plaques
- Chronic, destructive process
- Significant scarring

## Pathognomonic Sign
**"Apple-jelly" nodules on diascopy**
- Apply glass slide pressure to lesion
- Reveals gelatinous, brownish-yellow nodules under skin

## Long-term Complication
Risk of developing skin cancer in chronic lesions
