# Palatal Perforation - Differential Diagnosis

## Common Causes

### Substance-Induced
**Cocaine insufflation**: Most common in current era
- Ischemic necrosis
- Associated nasal septal destruction
- Key diagnostic clue for CIMDL

### Infectious
**Tertiary Syphilis**: Gumma formation
**Mucormycosis**: Rapidly progressive, black necrotic lesions
**Tuberculosis**: Rare palatal involvement

### Autoimmune/Vasculitis
**Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA)**: Exceptionally rare (despite frequent nasal involvement)

### Neoplastic
**Extranodal NK/T-cell Lymphoma**: Aggressive destruction

## Clinical Significance
**Palatal perforation in midline destructive lesions**:
- Strongly favors **CIMDL** over GPA
- CIMDL: >70 reported cases
- GPA: 5 poorly documented cases

## Associated Symptoms
- Nasal regurgitation of food/liquid
- Hypernasal speech
- Dysphagia
- Halitosis

## Examination Technique
**Must directly visualize palate** in all patients with:
- Nasal septal perforation
- Midline destructive lesions
- Chronic rhinosinusitis with destruction
